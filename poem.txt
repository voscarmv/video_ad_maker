 the melting clock dripped on my correspondence the letters sprouted tiny legs and scurried under the door a man made of newspapers recited yesterday's weather in a language of rust and static his hat was a boiling teapot and his briefcase contained a single forgotten constellation the cobblestones unfurled like tongues tasting the rain which fell upwards into the waiting clouds a woman with a face like a cancelled postage stamp offered me a key that unlocked a room that did not exist I unlocked it anyway and stepped into the cacophony of a silent film projected onto the writhing surface of the sea a gramophone needle trailed a fissure in the sky behind it audible geometry spilling out like entrails we ate the solid light with spoons made of bone
